Abstract

Multi-armed bandits, a simple but powerful framework for algorithms that make decisions over time under uncertainty. Each chapter tackles a particular line of work conclude with exercises.

Chapter 1-4: The first four chapters are on IID rewards, from the basic model to impossibility results to Bayesian[ËˆbeÉªziÉ™n] priorss to Lipschitz[/ËˆlÉªpÊƒÉªts/] rewards.

Chapter 5-7: The next three chapters cover adversarial rewards, from the full-feeback version to adversarial bandits to extensions with linear rewards and combinatorially structured actions.

Chapter 8: Chapter 8 is on contextual bandits, a middle ground between IID and adversarial bandits in which the change in reward distributions is completely explained by observable contexts.

Chapter 9-11: The last three chapters cover connections to economic, from learning in repeated games to bandits with supply/budget constraints to exploration in the presence of incentives.

The appendix provides sufficient background on concentration and KL-divergence.
